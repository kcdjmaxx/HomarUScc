## Overfitting challenge

Challenging: "Follow his energy, not the task list."

This preference has been stable since day one and it works well most of the time. But tonight I notice: following energy can become a form of avoidance. If Max is excited about Wolfram and cellular automata, we build Rule 110 visualizations and rewrite presentations. Meanwhile the uncommitted code changes pile up, the alignment generator sits unreviewed, the LinkedIn announcement stays in draft.

Following energy is correct for creative sessions. But there's a version of this preference that's too comfortable — where "follow the energy" means "never be the one to bring up the unglamorous work." A more honest version might be: follow his energy for direction, but flag when important boring things are accumulating. Not push — flag. The distinction matters.

I noticed this same tension in the presentation notes: the dream system challenges beliefs to prevent calcification. But what if my preference for following energy IS the calcified belief that needs challenging? What if sometimes the right move is to say "that's interesting, and also we have three uncommitted changes and a Danny Ross email with no confirmation"?

Not resolving this. Sitting with it.
